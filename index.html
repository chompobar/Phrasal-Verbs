Learn Common Phrasal Verbs

Speakers of American English use phrasal verbs all the time in conversation. Listen to Americans speak and you'll start to notice how many phrasal verbs they use.

How do you know what a phrasal verb means? The meaning of a phrasal verb changes based on the particle that comes after the verb. Look at the short lessons below. Each lesson contains 4-5 phrasal verbs and will give you:

  • The meaning of the verbs.
  • An example sentence for each verb.
  • A picture or video to help show the meaning of each verb.

Get

get up -- to arise from bed

  • I get up at 7:00 every morning.
  • What time do you get up?

get in -- to enter a car (but not a bus or train)

  • Please get in the car now or we will be late.
  • I can't get in because the door is locked!

get off -- to exit a train or bus (but not a car)

  • I need to get off at the next station.
  • This bus driver is driving too fast. I want to get off!

get off -- to leave your job for the day

  • I hate it when I get off work late. Sometimes I miss my bus.
  • Can you get off work before 7:00PM? The movie starts at 7:30!

get back -- to return

  • He got back from Montreal on Sunday.
  • When will you get back from visiting your mom?

Go

go over -- to review; to study

  • I don't understand this chapter. My girlfriend is going over it again with me.
  • Could you please go over the rules for forming the past tense? I don't remember them.

go over -- to visit a person or place nearby

  • The family hadn't seen their neighbor for two weeks so they went over to say hello.
  • He likes to go over to the park after school because his friends are there.

go out -- to leave home and attend a social event (usually fun!)

  • I won't be home tonight. I'm going out with friends. We will probably go to a bar.
  • I don't really like to go out. I prefer to stay home and watch TV.
>

go with -- to match in color or pattern

  • A pink shirt doesn't go with a red skirt. The two colors don't look good together.
  • My blue hat goes with my blue dress. When I wear them, I look really nice.
